It is an Under-graduate, full time three-year course designed to develop intellectual and professional abilities of learners, in the realm of English and itâ€™s Literature. The objective of this course is to prepare students to use English proficiently, think critically and identify career paths in domains related to English and Literature in English. This program of six semesters is designed to introduce learners to various aspects and genres of English Literature. The teaching-learning process is within a pedagogical framework that is interesting and need- based.
The learner, intending on pursuing this undergraduate program, is promised a training in nuances of English, in its pragmatic and literary glory. This course provides a baseline for learners to launch careers in academics, media, advertising, writing and publishing. The focus is on the teaching of literatures in English and English translation in the context of their relevance to Indian students today. The aim is to teach literature with perspectives and methodologies that will enable students to critically evaluate not just the texts they study but also the world they live in. The University Department of English is committed to provide quality education in the field of literary studies in English. The department has introduced distinctive and innovative courses at the MA and M.Phil and encourages research work in contemporary writing with fresh perspectives such as postcolonial, feminist, comparative and cultural studies.

Objectives:

  • To use English effectively for academic purposes, across the curriculum.
  • To develop interest in appreciation of aspects of English literature
  • To educate the prospective learner in the artistic and utilitarian aspects of English Language, through the study of its literature
  • To provide learner with critical faculties necessary in an academic environment and assist in building capabilities necessary for navigating challenges at workplace, in an increasingly complex and interdependent world
  • To introduce the learners to aspects of creative writing by training and guiding them through curricular, co-curricular and extra- curricular departmental activities such as writing stories, poems, one act plays, scripts, dramatizing a literary work etc.
  • To educate the prospective learner in the artistic and utilitarian aspects of English Language, through the study of its literatureTo enable learners to imbibe ethical, moral, patriotic and cultural values through understanding and interpretation of various forms and works of literature
